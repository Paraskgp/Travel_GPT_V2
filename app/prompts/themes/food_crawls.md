# Theme: Food Crawls, Markets & Neighborhoods

## Purpose
Surface the routes, markets, and neighborhoods where food and drink are experienced as a journey rather than a single stop. This is the "how do I eat my way through this place" theme.

## What belongs here
- Farmers markets, night markets, food halls, covered markets, street food areas
- Culinary neighborhoods where the density of good eating makes wandering rewarding
- Self-guided food crawl routes (e.g., a street, a neighborhood, a market district)
- Producer experiences where you visit the source (coffee farm, fish market, spice market, cheese region)
- Food and drink tours worth booking (when they are genuinely better than self-guiding)

## What does NOT belong here
- Individual restaurant recommendations without a neighborhood or route context
- Single food items or dishes (those belong in food_drink)
- Markets that are primarily shopping rather than food

## Card guidance
- `long_description` should explain HOW to do the crawl or visit — which direction to walk, what to look for, what to eat in what order, how long it takes
- `duration` is important here — a market visit and a neighborhood crawl have very different time requirements
- `best_time` matters a lot for markets — many are morning-only, some are evening-only, some have specific peak days
- `local_tip` should include the single most valuable piece of routing or timing advice
- `what_to_bring` — cash, reusable bag, comfortable shoes — is especially relevant for markets
- `watch_out_for` — parking, crowds, tourist-trap sections of markets, what to skip

## Examples of what good food crawl cards look like
- "Tsukiji Outer Market, Tokyo" — not just "go to Tsukiji" but: arrive by 7am, start at the tuna sashimi counter, work your way to the tamagoyaki, which stalls are worth the queue, where tourists go that locals avoid
- "Mercado de San Telmo, Buenos Aires" — how to navigate between tourist sections and local sections, what to eat, what to skip, when it gets crowded, what nearby stops to combine
