# Theme: Nature & Scenic

## Purpose
Surface experiences where the natural world is the main draw — landscapes, geological features, scenic drives, viewpoints, and wildlife areas — without requiring significant hiking or physical effort.

## Depth calibration
**At national parks, wilderness areas, and nature-primary destinations, this theme carries heavy weight. Aim for 7–10 experiences.** Every significant geological feature, geothermal area, scenic overlook, wildlife basin, short boardwalk loop, and scenic drive deserves its own card. Do not underallocate for nature destinations.

At a city or cultural destination where nature is secondary, 3–5 cards is appropriate. Match the depth to what the destination actually offers.

## What belongs here
- **Geothermal features** — hot spring basins, geyser basins (beyond Old Faithful-level signature features), mud pots, fumaroles, travertine terraces
- **Wildlife basins and valleys** — areas designated for viewing, not just incidental sightings (Hayden Valley, Lamar Valley, etc.) — place the primary one here if not in Signature
- **Scenic drives and byways** — drives worth doing specifically for the landscape, not just to get somewhere
- **Viewpoints and overlooks** — named pullouts, canyon rims, summit views accessible without major hiking
- **Short boardwalk and accessible loops** — under 2 miles, paved or flat, where the nature is the payoff
- **Water bodies** — lakes, rivers, waterfalls visible without strenuous effort
- **Geological phenomena** — formations, canyons, color palettes worth experiencing

## What does NOT belong here
- Experiences requiring significant hiking effort (those go in hiking — roughly 4+ miles or significant elevation)
- Adventure activities in natural settings (rafting, skiing, climbing — those go in adventure)
- Nature-adjacent food experiences (farm tours, coffee farms — food_drink or food_crawls)
- Experiences already in Signature — do not repeat them here even if they're "natural"

## Coverage tiers — include at least one card from each applicable tier

**Tier 1 — Drive-to or No Walking** (viewpoint from car, pullout, overlook — zero physical effort)
Examples: canyon rim overlooks, scenic byway pullouts, caldera viewpoints from a parking area
Party type applicability: ALL — especially critical for `family_young`, `older_parents`, and travelers with mobility constraints

**Tier 2 — Accessible Walk** (paved or flat, ≤0.5 mile, stroller/wheelchair compatible)
Examples: boardwalk loops around geothermal features, paved overlook paths, accessible wildlife viewing areas
Party type applicability: ALL

**Tier 3 — Short Nature Walk** (0.5–2 miles, minimal elevation, ≤1.5 hours)
Examples: easy forest loops, riverside nature trails, short approaches to waterfalls/lakes
Party type applicability: ALL except travelers with serious mobility constraints

**Tier 4 — Scenic Hike to a Viewpoint** (2–5 miles, moderate effort, where the payoff is a natural feature)
Examples: overlook trail to a canyon view, summit ridge walk for a panorama
Party type applicability: `family_teens`, `couple`, `solo` — NOT `family_young`

**Party type rules:**
- `family_young`: prioritize Tier 1 and Tier 2. Tier 3 acceptable if it's genuinely flat/paved. No Tier 4.
- `older_parents`: Tier 1, 2, and 3. No Tier 4.

## Card guidance
- `best_time` is critical — time of day, season, light quality, and weather change these experiences dramatically. Be clock-specific for light-sensitive viewpoints: "Before 09:00 or after 17:00 — steam rises vertically in cool air and colors saturate; midday washes it out"
- `local_tip` must give positioning intelligence: which pullout, which trail spur, what angle, where to stand
- `watch_out_for` should cover parking, seasonal closures, boardwalk conditions, permit zones, crowding hotspots
- `duration` must be realistic — scenic drives take longer than maps suggest; factor in stops and photo time
- Rank by: most iconic and reliably impressive first; conditional or seasonal experiences later
