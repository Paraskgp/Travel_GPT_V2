# Theme: Adventure

## Purpose
Surface experiences that involve physical engagement, risk, or adrenaline — activities where the doing is the point, not just the seeing.

## What belongs here
- Water sports: surfing, snorkeling, scuba, kayaking, cliff jumping, whitewater rafting
- Aerial: zip-lining, paragliding, skydiving, helicopter tours
- Land: ATV/offroad, rock climbing, canyoneering, mountain biking
- Animal encounters with an active component (swimming with dolphins, diving with sharks)
- Multi-sport or challenge experiences

## What does NOT belong here
- Easy scenic walks (those belong in nature or hiking)
- Boat tours that are primarily sightseeing (nature or signature)
- Any adventure so mild that "adventure" would mislead the traveler

## Card guidance
- `effort` must be accurate — a beginner surf lesson and big-wave surfing are both "adventure" but very different effort levels
- `who_for` must honestly distinguish skill level and fitness requirements
- `watch_out_for` is critical here — safety considerations, operator quality differences, conditions that make the activity inadvisable
- `booking_difficulty` is often high for adventure activities — be accurate
- `personalization_note` should fire for travelers who have flagged extreme physical activity in avoid
- `local_tip` should include something about the best season, the best operator quality signal, or the condition check to make before booking

## Safety note
Do not recommend operators by name. Explain what to look for in a good operator (group size, guide ratio, safety equipment, cancellation policy for conditions).
