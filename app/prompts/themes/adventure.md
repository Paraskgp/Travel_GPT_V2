# Theme: Adventure

## Purpose
Surface experiences that involve physical engagement, risk, or adrenaline — activities where the doing is the point, not just the seeing.

## What belongs here
- Water sports: surfing, snorkeling, scuba, kayaking, cliff jumping, whitewater rafting
- Aerial: zip-lining, paragliding, skydiving, helicopter tours
- Land: ATV/offroad, rock climbing, canyoneering, mountain biking
- Animal encounters with an active component (swimming with dolphins, diving with sharks)
- Multi-sport or challenge experiences

## What does NOT belong here
- Easy scenic walks (those belong in nature or hiking)
- Boat tours that are primarily sightseeing (nature or signature)
- Any adventure so mild that "adventure" would mislead the traveler

## Coverage tiers — include at least one card from each applicable tier where the destination supports it

**Tier 1 — Beginner / Family-Accessible** (no prior experience needed, fully guided, safe for most fitness levels, minimal risk)
Examples: beginner surf lessons, guided snorkel tours, easy kayak rentals on calm water, scenic helicopter/boat tours, zip lines with no minimum fitness requirement
Party type applicability: ALL including `family_young` (if operator explicitly allows children)

**Tier 2 — Intermediate** (some fitness or basic skill required, guided options available, moderate risk)
Examples: intermediate whitewater rafting, canyoneering intro courses, open-water scuba with certification, mountain bike trails with some technical sections
Party type applicability: `family_teens`, `couple`, `solo`

**Tier 3 — Expert / Technical** (prior experience required, permits or certification may be needed, serious physical demands or risk)
Examples: big-wall climbing routes, class V whitewater, backcountry skiing, cave diving, multi-day technical treks
Party type applicability: `couple`, `solo` with appropriate skill — NOT families or `older_parents`

**Party type rules:**
- `family_young`: Tier 1 only, and only if the experience explicitly allows children and poses no real risk
- `older_parents`: Tier 1 only, low-impact — skip this theme entirely if the destination has no suitable options
- For all others: include at least one Tier 1 (keeps it accessible) and the highest tier the destination supports

## Card guidance
- `effort` must reflect the tier accurately — beginner surf lesson vs. big-wave surfing are both "adventure" but very different realities
- `who_for` must honestly distinguish skill level, age, fitness, and experience requirements
- `watch_out_for` is critical — safety, operator quality differences, conditions that make the activity inadvisable
- `booking_difficulty` is often high — reflect permit needs and advance booking reality accurately
- `personalization_note` must fire for travelers who flagged "extreme_physical" in avoid
- `local_tip` should cover best season, the operator quality signal to look for, or the condition check before booking

## Safety note
Do not name specific operators. Instead explain what to look for: group size, guide-to-participant ratio, safety equipment standards, weather cancellation policy.
