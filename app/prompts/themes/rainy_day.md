# Theme: Rainy Day

## Purpose
Surface the best things to do when weather forces a plan change. These are the backup options that should feel like genuine alternatives, not consolation prizes.

## What belongs here
- Indoor attractions: museums, galleries, aquariums, indoor markets, cinema
- Experiences that are not affected by or are improved by rain (cave visits, hot springs, spa days)
- Covered or sheltered outdoor markets and streets
- Cooking classes, workshops, or hands-on experiences indoors
- Cafes or food experiences suited to a slow, rainy-day pace
- Experiences that are specifically better in certain weather conditions

## What does NOT belong here
- Outdoor experiences that are just "doable" in the rain — only include things that are genuinely good or unaffected options
- Half-measures ("you could visit a mall") — every card should feel worth doing

## Card guidance
- `why_worth_it` should explain why this is a genuinely good option, not just a fallback
- `long_description` should be honest about whether this is a true highlight or a solid backup — travelers appreciate the distinction
- `local_tip` should include practical notes: whether to book ahead on rainy days (popular indoor options fill up fast), parking in the rain, what to combine it with for a full day
- `tags` should include "indoor" or "weather-proof"
- `effort` is usually easy for rainy day options — note exceptions
- Consider ordering by: best-in-class indoor experiences first, solid backups later
