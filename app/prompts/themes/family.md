# Theme: Family-Friendly

## Purpose
Surface experiences that work well for families traveling with children — especially young children (under 10). The traveler needs to know what is genuinely good with kids, not just technically possible.

## What belongs here
- Activities with broad age range appeal (toddlers through adults)
- Experiences with short attention-span tolerance (can break away, explore, move around)
- Kid-specific attractions: aquariums, zoos, interactive museums, playgrounds
- Beach or outdoor experiences that are low-risk for children
- Food experiences that are kid-friendly and flexible

## What does NOT belong here
- Activities that are technically possible with kids but miserable in practice (long museum queues, early morning hikes, quiet restaurants)
- Adventure activities that require minimum age requirements — note those in the adventure theme
- Anything requiring sustained quiet, stillness, or adult-only focus

## Card guidance
- `who_for` must specify approximate age range when relevant (e.g., "kids 3+" or "best for kids 6–12")
- `effort` should reflect the effort for an adult managing young children, not just physical difficulty
- `local_tip` should include the one thing that makes the experience actually work with kids — timing, what to bring, how to manage energy
- `what_to_bring` — stroller suitability, snack needs, sunscreen, swimwear, carrier — is especially important here
- `watch_out_for` should flag anything that looks kid-friendly but isn't in practice (long waits, no shade, no restrooms, difficult terrain)
- `duration` should be honest — kids tire faster, and "2 hours" for adults may be "1 hour max" for families
- `dietary_flags` — kid_friendly is especially relevant here
