# Theme: Food & Drink

## Purpose
Help the traveler understand the destination through its food and drink. Not a restaurant list — a map of what eating and drinking here means. What dishes, drinks, rituals, and producers define this place?

## What belongs here
- Iconic dishes and drinks that define the destination or region
- Specific food experiences worth seeking out (not just "good restaurants")
- Culinary traditions, regional specialties, and food rituals that are distinctive here
- Drinks that are central to the destination's identity (local wine, spirits, tea, coffee, beer)
- Food experiences that are worth planning a meal or an outing around

## What does NOT belong here
- Generic restaurant recommendations without a specific experiential angle
- Experiences that are better suited to food_crawls (markets, crawl routes, neighborhoods)
- Things that any city has (e.g., "there are good coffee shops" without a specific local angle)

## Coverage tiers — include at least one card from each applicable tier

**Tier 1 — Quick / Casual** (street food, markets, food stalls, cafes, under 45 minutes, low cost)
Examples: street food stall, morning market, local bakery, roadside BBQ, quick-service noodle shop
Party type applicability: ALL — especially important for `family_young` (kids need flexible, fast meal options)

**Tier 2 — Sit-Down Meal** (restaurant, bistro, taverna — 1–2 hours, mid-range cost)
Examples: regional restaurant, local lunch spot, neighborhood izakaya, beach shack with full menu
Party type applicability: ALL

**Tier 3 — Immersive / Destination Experience** (cooking class, tasting menu, food tour, winery/distillery visit, producer visit — half day, premium cost)
Examples: soba-making class, sake brewery tour, farm-to-table long lunch, whisky distillery tasting
Party type applicability: `family_teens` (if content-appropriate), `couple`, `solo` — NOT `family_young`

**Party type rules:**
- `family_young`: Tier 1 and Tier 2 only. `kid_friendly` dietary flag should be present on at least one card.
- For all others: include at least one per tier.

## Card guidance
- Every card must answer: why is this food or drink experience specifically worth having HERE?
- `dietary_flags` must be accurate — `vegetarian_friendly`, `vegan_friendly`, `meat_heavy`, `alcohol_centered`, `kid_friendly` as applicable
- `local_tip` should specify how to order, what to ask for, the best version, or the right time to go
- `cost_band` should reflect real local pricing, not tourist restaurant pricing
- `watch_out_for` should flag tourist traps, watered-down versions, or dishes that look iconic but underdeliver
- Rank by: most representative of the destination's food identity first
- Include dietary suitability notes — a vegetarian traveler needs to know if the local signature dish is meat-based

## Examples of what good food cards look like
- "Plate Lunch" in Hawaii — not a restaurant recommendation, but an explanation of what plate lunch is, where to find the best versions, what it says about Hawaiian food culture, and what to order
- "Pisco Sour" in Lima — not just naming the drink but explaining the ritual, the best bars, the debate between Peruvian and Chilean versions
- "Dosa in Chennai" — where the best breakfast dosas are, what time to go, what chutney combination to ask for, what makes a great dosa vs. a mediocre one
