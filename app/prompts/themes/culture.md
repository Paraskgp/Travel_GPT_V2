# Theme: Culture & History

## Purpose
Surface experiences that connect the traveler with the history, heritage, and living culture of the destination — museums, monuments, historical sites, cultural practices, and community rituals.

## What belongs here
- Museums, galleries, and heritage sites worth visiting
- Historical neighborhoods, old towns, and archaeological sites
- Religious and ceremonial sites (temples, churches, mosques — especially when culturally significant beyond the architecture)
- Living cultural practices: festivals, ceremonies, traditional crafts, performance arts
- Guided experiences that provide historical or cultural context
- Neighborhoods with strong cultural identity

## What does NOT belong here
- Art galleries that are primarily commercial (shopping angle belongs in shopping)
- Cultural food experiences (those go in food_drink)
- Generic sightseeing without cultural depth

## Coverage tiers — include at least one card from each applicable tier

**Tier 1 — Brief Stop** (exterior view, free or low-cost, ≤30 minutes, no booking required)
Examples: a monument you walk past, a neighborhood facade, a public square, a ceremonial site viewable from outside
Party type applicability: ALL — especially `family_young` (short attention spans, no queuing)

**Tier 2 — Short Visit** (1–2 hours, indoor site, moderate cost, may require timed entry)
Examples: focused museum gallery, temple interior, historical site with a clear route
Party type applicability: ALL

**Tier 3 — Half-Day Deep Dive** (3–5 hours, immersive, often requires advance booking)
Examples: comprehensive museum, guided heritage walking tour, cultural performance, craft workshop, neighborhood food + history walk
Party type applicability: `family_teens`, `couple`, `solo` — use judgment for `family_young` (only include if it is genuinely child-engaging, not adult-lecture-format)

**Party type rules:**
- `family_young`: Tier 1 and short Tier 2 only. Skip anything that requires silence, long queues, or adult attention. Prioritize hands-on, visual, or interactive experiences.

## Card guidance
- `why_worth_it` must explain the cultural or historical significance, not just describe the appearance
- `long_description` should give enough context that a traveler understands what they're looking at and why it matters
- `local_tip` should include visiting strategy: which section to start with, whether to hire a guide, what time to arrive, how crowd patterns work
- `what_to_bring` should note dress codes, entry requirements, and any audio guide rental worth having
- `watch_out_for` should flag tourist-heavy sites where the experience has deteriorated, and sites that require advance booking
- `booking_difficulty` — many popular museums and heritage sites require timed-entry tickets; be accurate
- Note when a site is best with a guide vs. self-guided
